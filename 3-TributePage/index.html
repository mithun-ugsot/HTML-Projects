About Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was an American business magnate, industrial designer, and visionary who co-founded Apple Inc.. He was known for his creativity, leadership, and ability to bring innovation into consumer technology. Under his leadership, Apple introduced revolutionary products like the iPhone, iPod, iPad, and Macintosh computer.

Jobs’ focus on simplicity, design, and user experience transformed multiple industries — including personal computing, mobile phones, and digital media. His ideas continue to inspire millions across the globe.

Key Achievements

  • 1976 – Co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne.
  • 1984 – Launched the first Macintosh computer.
  • 1997 – Returned to Apple and revived the company from near bankruptcy.
  • 2001 – Introduced the iPod, changing the music industry.
  • 2007 – Revolutionized smartphones with the first iPhone.
  • 2010 – Unveiled the iPad, shaping the tablet era.
